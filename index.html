I'm a statistician currently working in infectious disease epidemiology at Imperial College London

I also maintain an active interest in privacy-preserving statistical and machine learning methods, particularly those involving Fully Homomorphic Encryption

Research

interests

I am interested in statistical and machine learning methodology and their biomedical applications:
  • Epidemiology and Biostatistics: My current research is focused on malaria epidemiology, specifically vector control monitoring [details; with Thomas Churcher]
  • Statitics and Machine Learning methodology: predictive modelling; computational statistics; functional data analysis; regularisation, penalization, dimension reduction and feature selection techniques for large (big data) problems; Bayesian modelling.
  • Computational Privacy: I also maintain an active interest in privacy-preserving statistical and machine learning methods, particularly those involving Fully Homomorphic Encryption — which was the topic of my PhD [details; with Chris Holmes and Louis Aslett]

software

  • EncryptedStats R package : statistical and machine learning methods for encrypted computation.

previous work

I have worked on mathematical modelling and stochastic population dynamics, namely the use of Bayesian state-space methods to understanding the dynamics of ecological populations [with Michael Bonsall and Órlaith Burke] and modelling the stochastic dynamics of the hematopoietic stem cell system [with Michael Bonsall]

Bio

background

I completed my PhD in Statistics at the University of Oxford as part of the Life Sciences Interface DTC, a 4-year doctoral programme of interdisciplinary research in the biomedical and life sciences, where I was supervised by Profs. Chris Holmes and Michael Bonsall. Previously, I studied Applied Statistics at the University of Oxford and Economics at Universidade Nova de Lisboa.
